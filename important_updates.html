Mass Expulsions Target
Major Clans on MVP SMP

📰 Internal Report MVP SMP · · ~3 min read
In a sudden and controversial move, the MVP SMP administration has executed a series of unilateral expulsions, effectively dismantling two of the server's most prominent groups: Kryzi's Clan and KinGExileD's Clan — with no prior warning, evidence, or disciplinary process.

Expulsion Without Evidence

Reports confirm that Co-owner KinGExileD was summarily dismissed from his position and the server without any evidentiary support. No investigation was conducted, no disciplinary hearing was held, and no reason was formally communicated.

Following this initial removal, a wave of expulsions swept through the server targeting high-activity personnel associated with both clans:

None of the removed individuals received prior notice or a formal disciplinary hearing. The removals were executed without documented evidence of misconduct.

"Out of Nowhere"

The removal of Kryzi's entire clan reportedly occurred without warning, following what sources describe as a simple misunderstanding and a promotion dispute involving a new staff member, Katelyn.

Internal logs reviewed by this reporter suggest that these removals were not based on individual player misconduct. Instead, the determining factor appears to have been group membership alone — a form of guilt by association that raises serious questions about the fairness of the administration's process.

"It came out of nowhere. Nobody got a warning, nobody got a chance to explain themselves. One day we were all there, the next — gone."

— Anonymous source, former MVP SMP member

The "Spy Hunt" Mentality

Perhaps most alarming is the atmosphere that appears to have developed within the administration itself. Staff members Nash Hie and LittleChungy were reportedly observed celebrating the mass removals, characterizing the purge as a successful "spy hunt".

This framing — treating loyal, long-standing community members as enemy infiltrators — reveals a shift in administrative culture from server management toward something closer to paranoia. When legitimate members are treated as suspects simply because of who they associate with, the social contract that holds any community together begins to collapse.

Most egregiously, the purge extended to members who were entirely inactive due to personal bereavement — including ThisOrca6994 — demonstrating that even circumstances of real human hardship offered no protection from the sweep.

🔍

Targeting inactive members who are absent due to personal grief is not server moderation — it is a symptom of what observers are calling "administrative paranoia", a pattern where leadership prioritizes control over community.

What This Means

These mass kicks mark what many believe are the final stages of the dissolution of MVP SMP's social foundation. The server's most active clans have been gutted, its co-owner expelled without cause, and its moderators removed without due process.

When an administration begins to eliminate its most engaged members — not for what they did, but for who they know — it signals the end of trust. And without trust, no server survives.

Eventually, due to the compounding stress and suffering caused by these events, both KinGExileD and ThisOrca made the difficult decision to leave not only MVP SMP — but Cloudrend SMP as well. Their departures leave behind an unsettling question that now echoes through both communities:

Who's the mole? 🕵️

The circumstances surrounding the purge — the precision of the targeting, the insider knowledge required to identify clan affiliations, and the speed of the removals — suggest that someone within the trusted circle may have been feeding information to the administration. Whether that suspicion is warranted remains unconfirmed, but the question itself is now impossible to ignore.

Cloudrend SMP's Response
